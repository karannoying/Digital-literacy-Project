# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

*Task 5 – Part A | CSE0001 Digital Literacy*

---

## What Is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud refers to criminal attempts to steal money from victims by exploiting the UPI payment ecosystem through social engineering, fake links, screen-sharing scams, or impersonation. Unlike traditional bank fraud, UPI fraud requires no physical card — just a phone number, UPI ID, and psychological manipulation.

---

## Realistic Scenario: The "Refund Scam"

**Background:**
Rohit, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, recently listed his old laptop for sale on OLX (an online classifieds platform). Within hours, he received a message from someone claiming to be an Army officer interested in buying the laptop.

**Step-by-Step: How the Fraud Unfolded**

1. **Initial Contact:** The fraudster contacted Rohit over WhatsApp, expressing urgent interest and offering to pay the full amount (₹18,000) immediately via UPI.

2. **Building Trust:** To appear legitimate, the person shared a fake military ID card image and spoke formally and confidently.

3. **The "Send Request" Trick:** The fraudster said, "I am sending you ₹18,000 now — please accept the request on PhonePe." He sent Rohit a *collect/payment request* — not a transfer — for ₹500, disguised as a "verification amount."

4. **Confusion Between Send and Receive:** Rohit, unfamiliar with UPI collect requests, entered his UPI PIN believing he was *receiving* money. He was actually *approving a debit* of ₹500 from his account.

5. **Escalation:** The fraudster repeated this trick three times, claiming "the system needs verification." Rohit lost ₹1,500 before realising nothing had been deposited.

6. **Disappearance:** The fraudster blocked Rohit immediately after.

---

## Who Is Typically Targeted?

- First-time UPI users (students, rural users, elderly individuals)
- People selling items on OLX, Quikr, or Facebook Marketplace
- Job seekers receiving "registration fee" requests
- People who have recently filed complaints and receive calls from fake "refund officers"

---

## Consequences

- **Financial loss:** Victims lose anywhere from a few hundred to several lakh rupees.
- **Psychological impact:** Shame, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital payments.
- **Legal complexity:** Recovering money from UPI fraud is slow and often unsuccessful if not reported within 24 hours.
- **Data exposure:** Victims who share screens (via AnyDesk/TeamViewer) may expose banking credentials, OTPs, and personal documents.
