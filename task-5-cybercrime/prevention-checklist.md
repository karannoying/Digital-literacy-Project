# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

*Task 5 – Part B | CSE0001 Digital Literacy*
*For college students in India*

---

## General Cyber Safety

- [ ] **1. Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or password with anyone** — not even someone claiming to be from your bank, NPCI, or a government office. No legitimate entity will ever ask for these.

- [ ] **2. Learn the difference between "Send" and "Receive" in UPI.** Entering your PIN always means *money leaves your account*. You never need a PIN to receive money.

- [ ] **3. Verify before you click.** Do not open links received via WhatsApp, SMS, or email claiming to be from banks, NPCI, or government portals. Go directly to the official website instead.

- [ ] **4. Do not install screen-sharing apps (AnyDesk, TeamViewer, Quick Support) at anyone's request.** Fraudsters use these to watch you enter your PIN or OTP in real time.

- [ ] **5. Use strong, unique passwords** for each platform (email, social media, banking apps). Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever available.

- [ ] **6. Keep your UPI apps and mobile OS updated.** Security patches fix known vulnerabilities that fraudsters exploit.

- [ ] **7. Do not scan QR codes sent by strangers** — especially when selling items online. Scanning a fraudulent QR code can initiate a debit from your account.

- [ ] **8. Be sceptical of "too good to be true" offers** — job offers, lottery wins, refund calls, or buyers offering to pay more than the listed price are common fraud triggers.

---

## UPI / Financial Safety (Specific Tips)

- [ ] **9. Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your bank's app or UPI settings. This limits damage in case of compromise.

- [ ] **10. Immediately block your UPI and call your bank if you suspect fraud.** Time is critical — report within 24 hours for the best chance of a reversal.

---

## How to Report Cybercrime in India

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File an online complaint 24/7 |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Call immediately if you have been defrauded (24x7) |
| **Local Police** | Visit your nearest police station and file an FIR for larger amounts |
| **Bank Fraud Reporting** | Call your bank's helpline immediately to freeze / reverse the transaction |

> **Remember:** Reporting quickly increases the chance of recovery. Do not be embarrassed — UPI fraud is common and designed to trick even careful people.
