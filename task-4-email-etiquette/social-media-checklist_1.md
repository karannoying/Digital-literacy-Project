# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

*Task 4 – Part B | CSE0001 Digital Literacy*

---

## DO's ✅

| # | Do This |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and a bio that accurately represents you as a student or aspiring professional. |
| 2 | **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor, future employer, or family saw this?" If not, do not post it. |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings actively.** Review and update your privacy settings on all platforms regularly. Limit who can see your personal information and posts. |
| 4 | **Give credit where it is due.** Always tag or mention the original creator when you share, repost, or build on someone else's content or ideas. |
| 5 | **Engage constructively.** Comment and reply in a respectful, thoughtful manner — especially in academic or professional communities like LinkedIn or GitHub. |

---

## DON'Ts ❌

| # | Avoid This |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Don't share personal information publicly.** Avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or Aadhaar/bank details anywhere on social media. |
| 2 | **Don't participate in or spread rumours.** Sharing unverified information — even as a joke or repost — can damage your reputation and harm others. |
| 3 | **Don't engage in or ignore cyberbullying.** Neither participate in online harassment nor stay silent when you witness it. Report and block offenders. |
| 4 | **Don't use social media during exams or classes to distract yourself or others.** It affects your focus and can be seen as disrespectful in academic settings. |
| 5 | **Don't post complaints about your college, professors, or classmates in a public forum.** Address grievances through proper institutional channels — this protects both you and others. |
