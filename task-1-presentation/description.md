## What the Design Shows

The infographic is presented as a 4-slide visual series, each covering a key aspect of digital literacy for engineering students:

| Slide | Title | Topics Covered |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Digital Literacy | Defines digital literacy as the ability to Find, Evaluate, Create, and Communicate using digital technologies |
| 2 | Useful Digital Tools for Engineering Students | Google Workspace, GitHub, MATLAB, Canva — with a short description of each tool's use in an engineering context |
| 3 | Safe Internet Habits | Use strong passwords, recognise phishing, not sharing OTPs, never share your network, use a secure Wi-Fi network, keep software updated |
| 4 | Professional Online Presence | LinkedIn tips (banner, achievements, skills) and GitHub tips (profile repos, detailed READMEs, commit history) |

## Reflection (150 words)

I used Canva to create this four-part infographic series as my Task 1 submission. Canva's drag-and-drop interface made it easy to work with icons, colour palettes, and layout grids without needing any prior design experience.

The most interesting part of this task was deciding how to structure the content visually. I initially tried to fit everything onto a single slide, but quickly realised that breaking the content into four focused slides made each topic much clearer and easier to read. Each slide has a specific colour theme — white for the definition, blue for tools, teal for internet safety, and steel blue for professional presence — which helps viewers immediately distinguish between sections.

The most difficult part was writing concise descriptions for each tool and tip. In engineering communication, being precise and brief is a critical skill, and this exercise gave me a useful early practice in that regard.
